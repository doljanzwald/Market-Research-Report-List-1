<p><ul><li>Radioactive isotopes</li><li>Non-radioactive labels</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The in-situ hybridization market is primarily categorized into two types based on labeling methods: radioactive isotopes and non-radioactive labels. Radioactive isotopes utilize radioactive materials for detecting specific DNA or RNA sequences, offering high sensitivity but requiring stringent safety measures. Non-radioactive labels, such as fluorescent or chromogenic markers, provide safer alternatives with easier handling and visualization. Both types serve critical roles in molecular biology, enabling researchers to study gene expression, localization, and cellular processes effectively while catering to varying requirements and lab conditions.</p></p>
