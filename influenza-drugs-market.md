<p><p>Influenza drugs are antiviral medications designed to prevent or treat influenza virus infections. These treatments include neuraminidase inhibitors such as oseltamivir (Tamiflu) and zanamivir (Relenza), as well as polymerase inhibitors like baloxavir marboxil (/Xofluza). The demand for influenza drugs is driven by seasonal outbreaks and potential pandemics, emphasizing the need for effective antiviral therapies.</p><p>The Influenza Drugs Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.6% during the forecast period, fueled by factors such as rising awareness about influenza vaccination, increased healthcare expenditure, and advancements in drug development. The emergence of antiviral resistance and the ongoing threat of pandemic influenza strains are also propelling market growth. Recent trends indicate a growing focus on combination therapies and the development of next-generation antiviral drugs, which aim to enhance efficacy and minimize resistance. Additionally, investments in research and development, along with collaborations between pharmaceutical companies and public health organizations, are expected to further accelerate market expansion. As the global awareness of infectious diseases increases, the influenza drugs market is poised for substantial growth in the coming years, aiming to address the evolving challenges posed by the influenza virus.</p></p>
