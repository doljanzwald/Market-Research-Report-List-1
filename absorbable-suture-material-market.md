<p><p>The absorbable suture material market is divided into two main types: synthetic and natural materials. Synthetic materials, such as polyglycolic acid and polylactic acid, are engineered for consistent absorption rates and strength, making them suitable for various surgical procedures. In contrast, natural materials, like catgut derived from animal intestines, are biologically sourced and have been traditionally used for their absorbability and biocompatibility. Both categories serve to minimize the need for suture removal while promoting healing in tissues.</p></p>
