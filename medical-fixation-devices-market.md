<p><p>The medical fixation devices market is categorized into internal and external fixation devices. Internal fixation devices, such as plates, screws, and pins, are surgically implanted to stabilize broken bones from within the body, promoting proper healing. In contrast, external fixation devices involve frameworks placed outside the body, using pins or screws to secure bones while allowing for accessibility and monitoring. Both types play crucial roles in orthopedic surgery, enhancing recovery outcomes and improving the overall quality of patient care.</p></p>
