<p><p>The Melamine Moulding Compounds market primarily encompasses two key molding types: injection molding and compression molding. Injection molding involves injecting molten melamine compounds into a mold, allowing for rapid mass production of complex shapes and designs, ideal for high-volume applications. In contrast, compression molding entails placing melamine paste into a heated mold where pressure shapes the material, suited for producing larger, thicker components. Both methods leverage the durability and heat resistance of melamine compounds, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
