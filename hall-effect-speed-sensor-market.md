<p><p>A Hall-effect speed sensor is a type of transducer that utilizes the Hall effect to detect the speed of rotating objects. It generates an electrical signal in response to the magnetic field associated with moving parts, commonly employed in automotive and industrial applications for monitoring rotational speed, position, and timing. The sensors offer high accuracy, reliability, and resistance to environmental factors, making them ideal for applications in motors, wheels, and gears.</p><p>The Hall-effect speed sensor market is witnessing notable growth driven by the increasing demand for advanced automotive safety features, including anti-lock braking systems (ABS) and electronic stability control (ESC).
