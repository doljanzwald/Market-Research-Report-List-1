<p><p>The trauma devices market includes various orthopedic fixation systems used to treat fractures and stabilize bone structures. Intramedullary nails are metal rods inserted into the marrow canal of bones. Cannulated screws have hollow shafts allowing for guided placement. Intramedullary hip screws and conventional hip screws secure femoral fractures, while staple fixation is used for bone stabilization. External fixations provide support externally to stabilize fractures, and bone pins are thin rods anchoring fractured bones together. Each device addresses specific clinical needs in trauma management.</p></p>
