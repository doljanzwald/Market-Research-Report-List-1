<p><p>RISC-V cores are categorized into two primary market types: RV32 and RV64, reflecting their 32-bit and 64-bit architectures, respectively. RV32 cores are ideal for low-power embedded applications, offering compact designs suitable for microcontrollers and IoT devices. In contrast, RV64 cores cater to high-performance computing needs, supporting larger memory addressing and complex applications in data centers and servers. Both architectures benefit from RISC-V's modular design, enabling customized configurations to address specific industry requirements.</p></p>
